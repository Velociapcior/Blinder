# Brainstorming Session — Blinder

**Facilitator:** Mary (Business Analyst)
**Date:** 2026-03-11

## Session Overview

**Topic:** Blind dating app — a chat-first dating platform where connection is established through conversation before any visual reveal

**Goals:** Generate ideas for features, mechanics, UX flows, differentiation strategies, and business model concepts for a complete app/website

### Problem Statement

Current dating apps (like Tinder) lead with profile pictures, causing users to judge potential matches purely on looks before any interaction. This filters out genuine compatibility signals and reinforces superficial connections. A blind dating platform inverts this model — conversation and personality compatibility come first, with visual reveal as a reward for connection, not a gatekeeper.

User is Piotr Palej. Building a complete app/website product. Aiming to cover the full product surface: core mechanics, UX, features, differentiation, and business model.

## Thematic Analysis

### Theme 1: The Reveal System
*Ideas: 1, 2, 3*

The three reveal ideas work together as a **layered reveal system** — not a single mechanism, but a configurable, consent-driven flow:
- Users choose their reveal pace (time OR message milestone)
- Neither party can be forced; mutual opt-in is the final gate
- This combination makes the reveal feel earned, consensual, and exciting — not arbitrary

**Insight:** The reveal is **the** core product differentiator. It needs to feel like a moment, not a button click.

---

### Theme 2: Matching Engine
*Ideas: 11, 14, 67*

No swipes. Matching is algorithmic, values-driven, and deliberately subversive:
- Quiz-based intake removes the browse-and-judge loop entirely
- Values alignment ensures meaningful compatibility signals before chat
- Anti-match introduces deliberate productive friction — challenging users' self-limiting preferences

**Insight:** The matching engine is the invisible engine of trust. Users never "browse" — they receive curated connections. This is philosophically critical to the brand.

---

### Theme 3: UX & Design Language
*Ideas: 31, 34, 36*

The product has a distinct aesthetic and spatial structure:
- **Cozy, warm UI** — feels like a café, not a marketplace
- **Max 3 active chats** — scarcity creates intentionality; you can't ghost everyone
- **Interest rooms** — discovery layer before 1-on-1; reduces cold-start anxiety for new users

**Insight:** The UX must reinforce the philosophy. Every design decision signals "this is not Tinder."

---

### Theme 4: Trust & Safety by Design
*Ideas: 44, 47*

Trust is built structurally, not just through policies:
- Post-reveal conversation ratings hold both parties accountable to quality interaction
- No boosts or super likes removes economic advantages — everyone competes on personality

**Insight:** These features are strong marketing proof points. "The only dating app where money can't buy you a better match."

---

### Theme 5: Brand & Philosophy
*Ideas: 49, 50*

Slow dating is a brand position, not just a feature set:
- Guided prompts embody the philosophy in the product (it actively helps you connect deeper)
- The slow dating market is underserved and growing — Hinge moved in this direction but didn't go far enough

**Insight:** "Blinder" is not just an app — it's a **movement** against superficial dating culture. The brand story sells itself.

---

### Theme 6: Business Model & Growth
*Ideas: 51, 70*

The monetization and reach strategy:
- Freemium with 1 match/day free keeps the product accessible while creating upgrade incentive
- International matching with translation is a massive TAM expansion — most dating apps are hyper-local

**Insight:** Real-time translation breaks the largest barrier to scale. Most competitors are English-only or language-siloed.

---

## Core Product Vision (Synthesized)

> **Blinder** is a slow dating app where you fall for someone's mind before you see their face.
>
> No swiping. No photo-first judgement. You're matched by values and personality, drop into a warm intimate chat environment, and choose together — when you're ready — to reveal yourselves. The only way to win here is to be genuinely interesting.

## Key Product Decisions to Make Next

1. **Reveal mechanics:** Are all three (time, milestone, mutual) available, or pick one as primary?
2. **Onboarding quiz:** How long? What values dimensions? Who designed it?
3. **Interest rooms vs. direct match:** Is the room discovery layer core or optional?
4. **Translation:** Real-time in-app or external API? Which languages at launch?
5. **Mobile-first or web-first or both?**
