## Session Overview

**Topic:** Blinder — A blind dating platform where conversation comes before appearance
**Goals:** Generate innovative features and mechanics that differentiate from Tinder/Hinge; serve underrepresented users (rejected, non-Instagram, overwhelmed); validate the "talk first, decide later" hypothesis

### Core Problem Statement

Mainstream dating apps fail a large segment of users:
- People rejected at first glance before they get a chance to connect
- Men mass-swiping out of desperation just to talk to someone
- Women objectified and flooded with low-quality messages
- "Instagram standard" beauty norms excluding real people
- Dopamine-loop design causing depression and low self-worth
- Quick hook-up culture drowning out people seeking real connection

### Core Hypothesis

When two people connect on a mental/personality level first, physical appearance becomes less of a barrier. Conversation is the real first date.

## Idea Organization

### Theme 1: Core Product Philosophy

**The founding beliefs that make Blinder different from every other app.**

- **Photo-First = Wrong Starting Point**
  The entire app inverts the assumption that visual attraction is the prerequisite for conversation. Conversation *is* the matching process. The photo reveal becomes a reward earned through genuine connection, not a gate.

- **Anti-Addiction by Design**
  Success means users spend *less* time on the app. Metrics are relationships started, not time-in-app. The app celebrates when you delete it. Marketing writes itself: *"The last dating app you'll ever need."*

- **Equal Treatment for All Users**
  No asymmetric gender mechanics. No pay-to-win. No exclusivity. Blinder is a safe space for everyone who feels rejected or overwhelmed — not a curated club.

- **The App Has One Job at a Time**
  When you're in a conversation, nothing else competes for attention. No "meanwhile, 3 new people joined!" banners. Full presence. One focus.

---

### Theme 2: Conversation Mechanics

**How conversations are structured, paced, and concluded.**

- **Core Mechanic #1 — The Conversation Window + Decision Gate**
  Two matched users talk within a defined window. At the end, both independently and simultaneously choose: **Reveal / Continue / Abandon**.
  - Mutual Reveal → profiles become visible to both
  - One chooses Continue → other gets Agree or Abandon choice
  - Any Abandon → conversation ends
  - Decision is simultaneous and private — neither knows the other's choice until both have chosen. Creates genuine tension and emotional investment.

- **Core Mechanic #3 — Dual-Trigger Conversation Window**
  The decision gate can be triggered two ways:
  - **Message threshold** (e.g. 50+ messages) unlocks an *early reveal option* — rewards high-energy conversations
  - **Time floor** (e.g. 24h) guarantees every conversation gets breathing room regardless of pace
  High-energy conversations can accelerate. Slow ones are protected. Emotionally intelligent pacing.

- **Core Mechanic #4 — Quiz-Seeded Conversation Starters**
  When a match begins, the app surfaces 1-2 conversation hooks derived from quiz answers — not "you both like movies" but something genuinely interesting. Removes blank-page anxiety without removing authenticity.
  No AI suggestions mid-conversation. Just a spark. People discover each other themselves.

- **Feature #1 — The Blind Conversation Archive**
  After a successful mutual reveal, the blind-phase conversation is preserved as a keepsake — accessible to both users forever. The story of how they met, before they knew what each other looked like. Real sentimental value. Costs little to implement, adds quiet emotional depth.

---

### Theme 3: Matching System

**How people get connected and how the queue is managed.**

- **Matching Model — Async Daily Match with Soft Start**
  Once per day Blinder finds your best available match in the background. You get a notification: *"Someone is waiting for you."*
  - The conversation window doesn't start until **both** users send their first message
  - No simultaneous activity required (easy to implement)
  - Match expires quietly after 48h if neither starts — both freed with no blame framing
  - Natural daily engagement rhythm without addictive notification loops

- **Matching Model — Conversation-Capped, Match-Free**
  No limit on daily matches received or passes made. The only limit is **active simultaneous conversations** (ones that have actually started).
  - Free tier: 1-2 active conversations
  - Premium: more active conversations
  - Pending matches that haven't started don't count against the cap
  - The paywall is on depth of engagement, not access to people

- **Matching Model — Symmetric Freedom**
  Either person can Pass on a pending match at any time → both freed immediately.
  Neither person is ever locked by someone else's inaction. The outcome of passing is anonymous — other person just sees match expired.

- **Assumption #5 — Algorithm Optimizes for Complementarity, Not Similarity**
  Psychiatrist-informed matching model (input from Piotr's sister, a psychiatrist): best matches are between people who complement each other, not people who are identical.
  Starting point: random matching. Long-term: develop complementarity algorithm.
  The algorithm's job isn't to predict attraction — it's to predict *conversation quality.*

### Theme 4: Emotional Safety Design

**How Blinder protects users from rejection, guilt, harassment, and low self-worth.**

- **Assumption #7 — Safety is Architectural, Not Reactive**
  Graphic content filtered at photo upload via automated moderation services. No photo sharing during conversations — text channel only. Safety isn't a report button; it's the product design itself.
  Marketable as: *"Zero harassment inbox"* — a genuine structural guarantee, not a promise.

- **Core Mechanic #5 — Outcome Anonymization**
  When a conversation ends without mutual Reveal, neither user is told who made which choice. Screen simply says: *"This conversation has ended."* No "they abandoned you." No blame. No author assigned to the ending.

- **Core Mechanic #6 — The "Good Conversation" Acknowledgment**
  Regardless of outcome, after a conversation ends both users see: *"Was this a good conversation?"*
  If both say yes → both are quietly told: *"You had a good conversation."*
  Decouples romantic success from human connection success. You can leave with dignity even if nothing continues.

- **Core Mechanic #7 — No Rejection Metrics**
  The app never shows abandon rates, match rates, or any statistic that functions as a personal score. No "you've been abandoned 7 times." The only visible progress is conversations had.

- **Core Mechanic #8 — Optional Cooldown**
  After a conversation ends, the app offers — not forces — a short break before the next match. Something like: *"Ready for your next conversation, or want a moment?"* The app cares without controlling.

- **Feature #3 — Match Expiry Without Shame**
  If a match expires because neither person started, both users see: *"This match has passed."* No guilt framing. No "you ignored someone." Time moved on.

### Theme 5: Profile & Onboarding

**How users are introduced to Blinder and how their identity is represented.**

- **Core Mechanic #2 — Psychologically-Crafted Onboarding Quiz**
  Profile built from a psychology-informed questionnaire — attachment style, communication patterns, conflict approach, values hierarchy. Designed with clinical input (psychiatrist perspective).
  Questions feel natural and intuitive to answer — no essay questions, no decision fatigue. You finish feeling heard, not processed.
  Not "what do you like" — but "who are you."
  Quiz should be fast enough that users get their first match quickly — they need to see value before investing heavily.

- **Post-Quiz Profile**
  Standard profile with profile picture shown after mutual reveal. MVP: simple and clean. No profile during blind conversation phase.
  Future consideration: profile section showing "what [Name] chose to share after talking to you" — personal reveal rather than generic profile. Not MVP.

---

### Theme 6: Visual & UX Design

**The emotional and aesthetic language of Blinder.**

- **Design Pillar #1 — Anti-Urgency Visual Language**
  Warm, muted colors. No aggressive reds. No countdown timers in alarm-clock fonts. No notification badges screaming. Soft typography. Generous whitespace. The app *breathes*.
  Every mainstream dating app is designed to create anxiety. Blinder is designed to lower it.

- **Design Pillar #2 — No Visible Counters or Scores**
  No "X people liked you." No match streaks. No daily login rewards. Nothing that makes you feel behind or ahead. Only your current conversation exists in the UI.

- **Design Pillar #3 — Cozy Safe Space Identity**
  Nothing on screen that would stress a user. Single focus. Calm. Intentional. The visual language communicates: *this is a different kind of place.*

---

### Theme 7: Business Model & Notifications

**How Blinder sustains itself and communicates with users.**

- **Business Model #1 — Conversation-Focused Freemium**
  Free tier: limited simultaneous active conversations, limited profile photos.
  Premium: more active conversations, more profile photos.
  The core experience is identical for free and premium users — premium gives more capacity, not better treatment.
  Honest pitch: *"You're not paying to meet people — you're paying to meet more people at the same time."*

- **Feature #2 — Meaningful Notifications Only**
  Blinder notifies you of exactly three things:
  1. A new match is ready
  2. Your conversation partner replied
  3. A decision gate has been reached
  Nothing else. No engagement bait. Every notification is signal, never noise.

## Brand Identity

**Name:** Blinder

"Blind" does triple duty:
- Blind date (the concept)
- Blind to appearance (the core mechanic)
- Removing the blinders that bias how we see people

The "-er" makes it active. You're not being blinded — you're choosing to see differently. The name explains the product, challenges the norm, and has emotional warmth. No explanation needed.

## Prioritization

### Must-Have (MVP Core)
1. Blind conversation mechanic (no photos during conversation)
2. Decision gate — Reveal / Continue / Abandon
3. Dual-trigger conversation window (time + message count)
4. Psychologically-crafted onboarding quiz with conversation starters
5. Outcome anonymization (no one knows who chose what)
6. Async daily match with soft start
7. Conversation-capped freemium model
8. Graphic content filtering at photo upload
9. Anti-urgency visual design language
10. Meaningful notifications only (3 types)

### Nice-to-Have (Post-MVP)
- The Blind Conversation Archive (keepsake of pre-reveal conversation)
- Optional cooldown offer after conversation ends
- Complementarity matching algorithm (psychiatrist-informed)
- "Good Conversation" acknowledgment mechanic

### Discarded / Not Pursuing
- Asymmetric gender mechanics → contradicts equal treatment principle
- AI coaching suggestions mid-conversation → too invasive, kills authenticity
- Forced cooldown → paternalistic, replaced with optional offer
- Exclusivity/paid barrier to entry → contradicts mission of inclusivity
- Living/dynamic profile → too complex for MVP

### Key Breakthroughs

1. **The reveal as emotional reward** — reframing the photo not as a starting point but as something earned through genuine human connection. This is the emotional core of the entire product.

2. **Safety through architecture** — Blinder doesn't need to moderate harassment it structurally prevents. This is both a design principle and a marketing claim.

3. **Psychiatrist-backed complementarity matching** — having clinical input on the quiz and algorithm is a genuine differentiator that no mainstream app has.

4. **Outcome anonymization** — nobody knows who made which choice. Guilt and blame disappear because there's no visible author to the ending. Simple and powerful.

5. **Conversation-capped freemium** — monetizing depth of engagement rather than access. Free users aren't second-class citizens. This is philosophically consistent with the mission.
